Our story

Why we built this

When our founder went to take the Nazari test in 2022, he failed on his first attempt. Not because he did not know how to drive. Because the test covers very specific Jordanian traffic law details, fines, and sign variations that you simply cannot guess your way through.

He searched for a preparation site. What he found were scattered PDF files, outdated question lists on forums, and apps that had not been updated in years. Nothing that felt like it was actually built to help someone pass.

"The test is not hard. Preparing for it was the hard part. We fixed that."

NazariPro launched in early 2024 with 200 crowdsourced questions and a basic mock exam. Within three months, students were sharing it on WhatsApp groups dedicated to driving license prep. The question bank grew to 850+ through submissions from students who used the site, passed, and came back to add the questions they remembered.

Today we are building toward a partnership with Jordan's driving schools and eventually toward an officially endorsed platform. The goal is not just a useful website. It is making Jordan's roads safer by making sure every driver actually understands the rules.

What we stand for

Our values

Honest about what we know
We are not the official government test. Our question bank is community-built. We say that clearly everywhere it matters.
Built for Jordan specifically
The Nazari test is different from generic traffic tests. We cover Jordanian law, Jordanian signs, Jordanian fines, and nothing else.
Arabic first
The real test is in Arabic. Our platform is Arabic-native. English is available because students asked for it, not because we defaulted to it.
Safety over revenue
We do not water down explanations to get more question volume. Every answer has a reason, because understanding why is what keeps people safe.
